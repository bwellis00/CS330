# Project Reflection

## 1. How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by first identifying the overall goal and then breaking the project into smaller, manageable parts. For this project, I focused on building the main scene first and then adding details over time.

### a) What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me improve my ability to design a complete scene using basic shapes and transformations. I became more comfortable with scaling, rotating, and positioning objects so they fit together in a balanced way. I also learned how lighting and color choices can improve the visual quality of a project.

### b) What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed a step-by-step process. I began with the larger objects, such as the cabinet, wall, and floor. After the main structure was complete, I added smaller details like the dartboard rings, divider lines, and mounted darts. Once everything was in place, I adjusted lighting and proportions until the scene looked finished.

### c) How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

These tactics can be used in future projects by starting with a simple foundation and improving it gradually. Breaking large goals into smaller tasks makes software easier to build and troubleshoot. This same process can be applied to websites, applications, or other programming projects.

## 2. How do I approach developing programs?

I approach developing programs by building one feature at a time and testing often. I prefer to make steady progress in small steps instead of trying to complete everything at once.

### a) What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One strategy I used was reusing code for repeated objects. After creating one dart, I duplicated it by changing positions and transformations instead of rewriting the full object. I also used trial and error by making small value changes, testing the results, and refining them.

### b) How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was a major part of this project. Many features were adjusted multiple times, including cabinet dimensions, dart placement, lighting angles, and dartboard details. Each revision improved the final scene and helped solve visual issues.

### c) How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?

Throughout the milestones, my approach became more organized and confident. Early on, I focused mainly on getting the code to run. Later, I became more comfortable improving structure, reusing code, and polishing the final product instead of only focusing on functionality.
